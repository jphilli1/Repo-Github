Why Normalize? Standard loan metrics include segments that vary dramatically across banks (consumer auto, credit cards, C&I lending, construction). Excluding these creates an "apples-to-apples" comparison focused on Private Bank/Wealth Management-style lending.

Peer Groups:

  • Core PB: Goldman Sachs + UBS (pure private bank peers)
  • All Peers: All comparison banks excluding MSPBNA

Excluded Segments:

  • C&I: Commercial & Industrial loans (varies by business model)
  • Fund Finance (NDFI): Capital call lines to PE/VC funds (specialty product)
  • ADC: Construction/Development loans (high-risk, varies by region)
  • Credit Cards: Unsecured consumer revolving (not PB-focused)
  • Auto: Consumer auto loans (mass market product)
  • Agricultural: Farm/Ag loans (regional specialty)
  • Owner-Occupied CRE: Business purpose real estate (C&I-adjacent)

Normalized View Includes: SBL (Securities-Based Lending), Residential Mortgages, Investment CRE, and Other Consumer loans typical of Private Bank portfolios.
